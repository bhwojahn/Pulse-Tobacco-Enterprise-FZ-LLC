Flue Cured Virginia

Characteristics

Bright tobacco with high sugar content, known for its smooth aroma and consistent burning qualities. Light to medium yellow-orange color with thin to medium body.

Use Cases

Primary component in cigarette blends worldwide. Also used in pipe tobacco and rolling tobacco. Provides sweetness and smoothness to blends.

Main Sourcing Regions

United States (Virginia, North Carolina), Brazil, Zimbabwe, Argentina, Tanzania, and China.

Air Cured Burley

Characteristics

Light to dark brown leaf with low sugar content and high nicotine levels. Air-cured in barns for several weeks, creating a robust, full-bodied flavor profile.

Use Cases

Essential for American blend cigarettes, provides body and strength. Used in pipe tobacco, chewing tobacco, and modern smokeless products.

Main Sourcing Regions

United States (Kentucky, Tennessee), Malawi, Brazil, Argentina, and Mexico.

Dark Air Cured

Characteristics

Dark brown to almost black leaves with heavy body and strong flavor. Low sugar, high nicotine content. Cured slowly in the absence of sunlight.

Use Cases

Traditional for chewing tobacco, snuff, and cigars. Used in specific regional cigarette blends and traditional pipe tobacco mixtures.

Main Sourcing Regions

United States, Italy, Indonesia, Philippines, and select African countries.

Dark Fire Cured

Characteristics

Distinctive smoky aroma from curing over open fires. Dark color with robust, full flavor and low sugar content. Creates a unique aromatic profile.

Use Cases

Specialty component in snuff and chewing tobacco. Used in premium pipe tobacco blends for smoky character. Regional cigarette blends in specific markets.

Main Sourcing Regions

United States (Kentucky, Tennessee, Virginia), Italy, and select traditional production areas.

Oriental

Characteristics

Small aromatic leaves sun-cured to develop distinctive fragrance. Highly aromatic with low sugar and nicotine. Golden to light brown color with intense aroma.

Use Cases

Critical component in premium cigarette blends for aromatic quality. Essential for Turkish and Balkan-style tobacco products. Adds complexity to pipe tobacco.

Main Sourcing Regions

Turkey, Greece, Bulgaria, North Macedonia, and select Middle Eastern regions.

Stems

Characteristics

The central vein and stem portions separated from tobacco leaves during processing. Lower nicotine content than leaf, fibrous texture. Processed and cut to specification.

Use Cases

Reconstituted tobacco products, expanding tobacco, filler material in cigarette production. Used in paper production and as biomass for energy generation.

Main Sourcing Regions

Available from all major tobacco processing facilities globally as a byproduct of leaf processing.

Fines

Characteristics

Small leaf particles and dust created during tobacco handling and processing. Variable composition depending on source tobacco type. Contains all leaf components in reduced size.

Use Cases

Reconstituted tobacco sheet production, expanding tobacco manufacturing. Used in homogenized tobacco products and as raw material for tobacco extract production.

Main Sourcing Regions

Available from all tobacco processing and manufacturing facilities as a natural byproduct of operations.
